Figure Figure

1 - Donner d’un élément, une représentation qui en rende perceptible (surtout à la vue) l’aspect ou la nature caractéristique.
2 - Façon dont un élément individualisé et structuré détache sur ce qui l’entoure.
3 - Personnage célèbre ou personnalité marquante.
4 - Carte sur laquelle est représenté un personnage (roi, dame, valet).
5 - Unité linguistique ou disposition d’unités linguistiques comportant un écart sensible par rapport à la norme ou l’usage.
6 - Enchaînement de mouvement formant un tout (on distingue les figures libres et les figures imposées).
7 - Dessin servant à visualiser certains êtresmathématiques et permettant, en particulier en géométrie, d’éclairer une démonstration.
8 - Partie du corps considérée par rapport aux expressions qu’on y lit.
9 - Toute illustration (schéma, dessin, photo) dans un ouvrage. (Abréviation : fig.).

Figure Figure

1 - A person seen indistinctly or from a distance.
2 - To reach an understanding of a person’s actions, motives, or personality.
3 - To have a significant part or role in a situation or process.
4 - A person of a particular kind, especially one who is important or distinctive.
5 - A movement or series of movements following a prescribed pattern and often beginning and ending at the same point.
6 - To think, consider, or expect to be the case.
7 - To finally understand something or someone, or find the solution to a problem after a lot of thought.
8 - A shape which is defined by one more lines in two dimensions, or one or more surfaces in three dimensions, either considered mathematically or used as a decorative design.
9 - A diagram or illustrative drawing, especially in a book or magazine.
